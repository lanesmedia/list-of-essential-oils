What are essential oils?

An essential oil is the concentrated, aromatic compounds in plants that give them their properties. While there are many different essential oil types and combinations, the ones most commonly used are Lavender, Eucalyptus, Oregano, Tea Tree, and Peppermint.In essence, essential oils are natural volatile liquids found in plants, and they are often used for their medicinal properties. Its important to note that essential oils should never be ingested, and they should always be used in a diluted form when used for aromatherapy. Many people use essential oils to make their homes smell nice or even to help with health problems.

How to Store Your Essential Oils?

When storing your essential oils, it is important to keep them out of direct sunlight and in a dark, cool place. If you have a dark, cool place, a drawer in your linen closet is a good option for essential oil storage.If you are storing your essential oils in a dark, cool place, they should last for a few months. However, it is recommended that you use them within 6 months to a year.

What are Essential Oils Good For?
Essential oils are used to promote health and well-being. There is a growing interest in the use of essential oils for a variety of purposes, including aromatherapy, massage, and skin care. Here are some popular essential oils to use:
  • Lavender: Often used for relaxation and stress relief, lavender essential oil can also be used to soothe minor cuts and scrapes.
  • Peppermint: Peppermint essential oil is invigorating and refreshing. It can also be used to relieve headaches and nausea.
  • Eucalyptus: Eucalyptus essential oil has a fresh, minty aroma. It can also be used to relieve muscle pain and tension.
If you're new to essential oils, it's best to start with a small bottle of each oil. That way, you can experiment with different combinations of oils to find what works best for you.
